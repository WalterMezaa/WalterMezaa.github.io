1. Overview of Capturing Your Screen on iPhone 13 Pro#

The iPhone 13 Pro users can capture their screen with ease by simply pressing the Power and Volume Up buttons simultaneously. A camera shutter sound will be heard and a preview of the screenshot will be displayed on the bottom left portion of the screen. To open the screenshot, users may tap on the preview and then share it with ease with the help of the share icon. Additional editing options for the screenshot can be found when users tap on the image. Such editing options include adding more annotations and cropping the image. Additionally, users may also choose the desired output format for their screenshot inside the editing page.

2. Setting Up Capturing Your Screen#

To capture your screen, press and hold both the Power and Volume Up buttons together. You will hear a camera shutter sound indicating that a screenshot has been successfully taken. To review your screenshot, you can quickly view a preview of it on the bottom left of your screen. If you wish to share or edit the screenshot, simply tap the preview to open up the screenshot, and then click on the share icon for more options. Additionally, you can also make use of the Edit tool to crop your screenshot and highlight certain areas.

3. Capturing Your Screen#

After pressing the Power & Volume Up buttons, you can quickly edit your screenshot with tools like crop, mark-up, and more. Once you have edited your screenshot, you can save it to your Photos section. Additionally, you can also save your screenshot to your iCloud Drive or share it directly from the editor. To delete your screenshot, drag the preview of the screenshot that appears on the bottom left of your screen to the right and wait for it to disappear.

4. Troubleshooting Capturing Your Screen#

If the screenshot doesn’t show in the lower left corner, this could mean that you didn’t hold down the buttons long enough. Another possibility is that your device hasn’t been updated and is running on an old operating system or software version. To update your device, you can open the Settings app and tap General and then Software Update. Additionally, if you are using a case with your phone, then the buttons may not be able to be pressed correctly, so try removing it. Lastly, make sure that you don’t have any stubborn applications running on the background as this could be preventing you from taking a screenshot.
