Guide Recording Android Audio

  1. Install the AZ Screen Recorder app on your Android device.
  2. Connect your Android device to your computer via USB cable.
  3. Enable USB debugging on your Android device.
  4. Install the Android SDK platform tools on your computer.
  5. Open Command Prompt on your computer and navigate to the Android SDK platform tools folder.
  6. Type ‘adb devices’ and press enter.
  7. When the device is detected, type ‘adb shell screenrecord’ followed by the desired screen recording parameters.
  8. To record with audio, add the ‘–audio-source=mic’ flag at the end of the command.
  9. When the screen recording has finished, type ‘Ctrl + c’ to end the recording session.
  10. Transfer the recorded file from the Android device to your computer.

1. Introduction and Prerequisites#

The USB debugging mode must also be enabled on the Android device. To do this, open the Settings app and click on System -> About Phone. Scroll down and tap on the Build Number seven times, then go back to System -> Advanced -> Developer Options and enable the USB debugging option. Connect the Android device to the PC, then open the Command Prompt and navigate to the Platform Tools folder. Type in ‘adb devices’ and press Enter—if the device is detected, you are ready to go.
