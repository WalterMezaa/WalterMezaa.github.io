Take Screenshot Easily MacAir

  1. Click the Command+Shift+4 keys on your Macbook Air’s keyboard.
  2. You will see a crosshair appear on the screen.
  3. Drag the crosshair to select an area of the screen you wish to capture.
  4. As soon as you let go of the mouse button, a screenshot of the selected area will be taken and saved to the Desktop by default.
  5. To save the screenshot to the clipboard instead of the desktop, press the Control Key while dragging the selection.
  6. If you want to capture a specific window, press the Space bar after selecting the crosshair.
  7. You will see that the crosshair turns into a camera icon.
  8. To select a specific window, position the camera icon over it and click the mouse button.
  9. When the area or window is selected, the screenshot will be taken and saved to your Desktop by default.

1. Introduction to Screenshots#

Screenshots are useful for capturing images of information from your computer screen for various purposes, such as to share with a friend or preserve for future use. Taking screenshots on a Macbook Air is a simple process that anyone can master with a few keystrokes. In this guide, we will walk through the steps of taking a screenshot on a Macbook Air.

2. Understanding the Different Screen Capturing Functions#

MacBook Air offers two different screenshot functions.Firstly, you can take a screenshot of the whole screen by pressing the Command + Shift + 3 keys. This will take a snapshot of the entire screen and save it as an image file on your desktop. Secondly, you can take a screenshot of a certain area of the screen. To do this, press the Command + Shift + 4 keys. This will bring up a crosshair which you can then drag to select the area that you would like to capture. After releasing the mouse button, the selected area will be saved as an image file either to the clipboard or to the desktop.

3. Taking a Screenshot on a MacBook Air#

The selected area will be highlighted in gray, allowing you to confirm the screenshot area you have chosen. You can also press the Spacebar to take a screenshot of the current window, and your desktop will be included in the screenshot. You can also press the ‘Option’ key to capture the entirety of a scrolling window or web page. If you need to resize the selection area, you can click and drag the corners of the box to adjust it. Once you have your desired screenshot area, press the Enter or Return key to capture the image.

You can also press Command+Shift+3 instead of Command+Shift+4 to take a screenshot of your entire screen. You can also press Command+Shift+3+Control to take a screenshot of the Touch Bar if you have a Macbook Pro with Touch Bar.

Finally, the screenshot taken using any of these methods will be saved to the Desktop by default, or to the clipboard if you press the ‘Control’ key while taking the screenshot. You can also press the ‘Spacebar’ after taking the screenshot to enable a ‘Drop Shadow’ effect. If you need to locate the screenshot you took, it will be in the PNG format and will have the title “Screen Shot [date] at [time].png.

4. Tips for Taking Better Screenshots#

  1. Make sure that you move the crosshair where you exactly want the screenshot to be taken. You can drag it to select the area you want, or press the Spacebar and move the crosshair over the window to take the screenshot of the window.

  2. You can also take a screenshot of a menu without the title by pressing Shift+Command+4 then Space. This will make the crosshair turn into a camera icon.

  3. You can also use the Command+Control+Shift+3 shortcut to take screenshots of your entire screen. This will take a picture of your entire display and will save it to your Desktop.

  4. To save the screenshot automatically to the clipboard instead of to the Desktop, use Command+Shift+Control+4 instead. This is helpful if you want to quickly paste it in an email or an image-editing program.

  5. For more options on taking screenshots, use Command+Shift+5 to access Screenshot Utility. This utility gives you more options, such as a timer for delaying the screenshot, the ability to draw on the screenshot, or the ability to capture a video.

  6. Lastly, you can also take screenshots with third-party apps like Lightshot or Snagit. These apps give you more flexibility and features when taking screenshots, such as the ability to blur or pixelate parts of the screenshot, or quickly share them to social media.

Your cursor will turn into a camera icon and you can click on the window that you want to capture. The screenshot will be saved to the desktop as a PNG file.

Q4. Where do screenshots get saved on MacBook Air? A4. Screenshots taken on MacBook Air are saved to the desktop as a PNG file.

Q5. Can I take a screenshot of the Touch Bar on MacBook Air? A5. Yes, it is possible to take a screenshot of the Touch Bar on MacBook Air. To do this, press Command + Shift + 6 at the same time. The screenshot will be saved to the desktop as a PNG file.
