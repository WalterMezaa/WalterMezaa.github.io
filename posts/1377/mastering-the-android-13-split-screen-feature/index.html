Step By Step Guide to Splitting Your Android 13 Screen

Split Screen Android 13 Easy Android users have been waiting for a native split screen feature, and with the introduction of Android 13, this is now available. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to effectively use the new split screen feature.

First, open up two apps side-by-side. To do this, open the application switcher with a long press on the Recents button at the bottom of your screen. Then drag and drop one app on top of the other. Your two apps will now be side-by-side.

Next, adjust the size of the apps. To do this, simply drag and drop the divider line between the apps to resize them. This is a handy way to make the most of your screen real estate.

The split screen feature also allows for easy multitasking. While one app is in focus, you can still use the other app in the background. For example, you can watch a video while also sending an email.

Finally, you can close the split screen mode when you’re done. Simply drag and drop the app you don’t need anymore to the bottom of the screen. It will be closed and the remaining app will take up the whole screen.

The Android 13 Split Screen Feature provides a great way to make the most of your phone’s screen, and with this guide, you’ll have no trouble mastering it.

1. Understanding the Split Screen Feature on Android 13#

Android 13 comes with a brand new split-screen feature that can be accessed with a long press on the app-switcher icon. After pressing and holding on the icon, the app switcher opens, allowing you to drag and drop one app on top, and use two apps side-by-side. The new multi-window mode also allows you to set the size of your apps, depending on how much of the screen you want to use for each app.

The split-screen feature also has a new gesture to make navigating through different apps easier. You can simply swipe up on the app switcher to switch between the two apps on the split screen. This will make it easier to quickly switch between apps.

The split-screen feature works with all your favorite Android apps, and you can even open two of the same app side by side. For example, if you want to compare two versions of a document, you can open them both and have them side-by-side on the screen.

Using the split-screen feature also allows you to take advantage of the new quick settings feature. This feature allows you to quickly access and adjust different settings for your apps, such as switching between windowed vs full-screen mode.

The split-screen feature also makes it easier to multitask on your Android 13 device. You can now have multiple apps open at once and switch between them quickly with the new gesture. This feature makes it easier to get more done in less time. Additionally, you can use the split-screen feature to keep certain apps running in the background while you work on other tasks.

2. How to Activate the Split Screen Feature on Android 13#

  1. To enable the split screen feature on Android 13, you can long press the recent app button to open the app switcher.
  2. Once the app switcher is open, choose a window to be the upper window and drag it to the top part of the screen.
  3. The bottom part of the screen will now display a list of apps that can be run side-by-side with the upper window.
  4. Choose the app you would like to run alongside the one you’ve already placed and drag it to the bottom.
  5. Android 13 will now display both apps side-by-side in a split screen.
  6. To exit the split screen mode, simply drag and drop one of the windows to close both windows.
