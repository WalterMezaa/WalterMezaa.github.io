The most popular key for capturing screenshots is the Print Screen key. On most full-sized keyboard, it’s usually found at the top right side, often abbreviated to “PrtScn” or “PrntScrn”. It’s also likely to be accompanied by other keys like “Insert” and “Scroll Lock”. You can press the Print Screen key by itself to capture an image of your entire display or you can press it in combination with other keys to capture specific windows.

Capture the Entire Display

The simplest way to capture a screenshot of your display is to press the “Print Screen” (often abbreviated to “PrtScn”) key. It’s usually located at the top right side of the keyboard and may be accompanied by other keys like “Insert”, “Scroll Lock” or “Fn”. Press the key once to capture an image of your entire display.

Capture a Specific Window

For a screenshot of a specific window, press Alt + Print Screen. This key combination will capture an image of the active window and store it on your clipboard. You can then paste the image to your favorite image editing program and save it as a file.

IV. Exploring other Keyboard Shortcut Alternatives#

You can also try several keyboard shortcuts to capture screen in Windows. In addition to Print Screen key, there are also other shortcuts you can use to capture and print screenshots. Here are five of them:

First is Alt + Print Screen. This takes a screenshot of the active window rather than the whole screen. It’s great for when you want to capture a specific window and nothing else.

The second is Ctrl + Print Screen. This captures a copy of the whole screen to the clipboard. You can then paste the screenshot to a document or presentation in any program where you can edit the screenshot.

Third is Fn + Windows + Print Screen. This shortcut is available on some laptops and keyboards. It captures the whole screen and saves it to the “Screenshots” folder on your computer.

Fourth is Windows + Volume Down. This is another key combination available only on some computers. Pressing Windows + Volume Down will take a screenshot of the entire screen and save it to the Pictures -> Screenshots folder.

Lastly, there’s the Snipping Tool. It’s a utility tool included with Windows that you can use to take screenshots of individual windows instead of the entire screen. It also has other features that let you edit the screenshot before you save it.
