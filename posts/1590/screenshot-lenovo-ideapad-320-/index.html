1. Introduction to Screenshots#

Taking a screenshot is a great way to capture information to share with friends and family or to save for reference on your laptop. It is especially useful for Lenovo IdeaPad 320 laptop users to take screenshots of their computer screens, and here is a guide on how to do it in three easy steps.

The first step is to press the Print Screen button on the Lenovo IdeaPad 320 laptop. This key is usually located to the right of the F12 key. When you press it, the screenshot of your laptop screen will be saved to the clipboard.

The second step is to open an image editor, such as Microsoft Paint, and paste the screenshot from the clipboard. On Windows machines, use the keyboard shortcut “Ctrl + V” to paste the screenshot.

The third and final step is to save the screenshot. This can be done by clicking the “Save as” or “Export” button at the top of the screen. From here, you can rename and save the file in a variety of image formats, including PNG, JPEG, and BMP.

You can also choose to share the screenshot directly using email, instant messaging, or social media. Select the “Share” option, and your screenshot will be ready to go.

Using the Print Screen button and a photo editor, you can take screenshots of your Lenovo IdeaPad 320 laptop quickly and easily. In a few simple steps, you can save and share whatever you’re viewing on your screen in no time.

  1. Firstly, press the “Print Screen” button that is located on the top right of your Lenovo IdeaPad 320 laptop. This will capture your entire laptop screen, and store it in your clipboard.

  2. Open your preferred editor software (such as Microsoft Paint) and press “Ctrl” + “V” to paste the image from the clipboard.

  3. You can now save the screenshot as an image file, to your desired location. Alternatively, you can also use the editor to crop, resize or edit your screenshot.

3. Taking the Screenshot#

  1. Taking the Screenshot - After determining the area you want to capture, it is time to take the screenshot. Press the Print Screen button on your laptop keyboard, and the image you selected will be saved to your clipboard. Then, open up a photo editor such as Microsoft Paint, and Paste the Screenshot into the editor. You can do this by pressing Ctrl + V on the keyboard. Additionally, you can also Paste the screenshot directly into other programs such as Word, or into your browser. This will ensure that the screenshot is saved where you want it to be.

4. Saving & Editing the Screenshot#

Saving the Screenshot: Once the Print Screen button is pressed, the picture of the computer screen can be saved. It is useful to save this file in a separate folder for easy access. To do this, simply create a folder in the File Explorer on the computer and save the picture here.

Editing the Screenshot: Double click the screenshot to open it in an image editor. Microsoft Paint is a great free option for simple screenshots. Here you can crop, resize, add text or drawings, and make other changes to the screenshot.

Pasting Image to Different Applications: Pasting a screenshot is a process that requires a few simple steps. Most applications that allow pasting, like Microsoft Word, require the user to press CTRL+V on the keyboard while the cursor is in the area of the document where the image is to be inserted.

Making Edits to the Screenshot: Once the screenshot is opened in an image editor, users have free reign to make edits. This may include cropping, resizing, adjusting brightness or saturation, adding shapes or text, and much more. Make sure to save the edited image as a separate file to avoid overwriting the original.

Image File Formats: When saving the screenshot, users can select from many different image file formats. JPEG, GIF, PNG, and TIFF are all popular file extensions used in photography and web design. The right choice depends on the desired image quality and the amount of compression that is desired.

Batch Processing: For users with a lot of screenshots to process, tutorials can often become tedious. To make this process more efficient, programs like Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Lightroom can be used to batch process screenshots. Using batch processing, users can save a lot of time while still achieving professional results.
