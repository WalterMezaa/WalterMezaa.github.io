Take Screenshot on Mac

  1. Use The Keyboard Shortcut: One of the quickest and easiest ways to capture your screen on an Apple laptop is to use the keyboard shortcut Command + Shift + 3. When you hit this combination of keys, the entire screen will be captured and the image saved as a PNG on your desktop.

  2. Use Grab: Grab is an application that comes pre-installed on your machine and provides several screenshot options. To launch the application, find it in the Applications folder under Utilities. When the application is running, you can click the Capture tab and choose between capturing the entire screen, a window, or a user-defined selection.

  3. Use Preview: Preview is also an application that comes pre-installed on your machine. You can open it up from the Applications folder and select File > Take Screenshot. From here, you can view and save your desired shot. Additionally, Preview allows you to do some basic editing and add annotations to the screenshot.

2. The Quickest Way: Capturing with Keyboard Hotkeys#

The Quickest Way to Capture your Screen with Keyboard Hotkeys is to press Command + Shift + 3. This will take a screenshot of the full screen and store the image on your desktop. You can also press Command + Shift + 4 to take a selection-only screenshot of any area of your screen. By pressing the spacebar after this combination you can take a screenshot of any window. Lastly, pressing Command + Control + Shift + 3 will take a screenshot of the whole screen and copy it to the clipboard, ready for pasting wherever you need it.

The second way to capture your screen is with the Grab application. This application will give you access to take multiple types of screenshot such as timed screen, selection, window, and full screen. You can access this application by simply searching ‘Grab’ in your spotlight search.

Lastly, you can also use your MacBook’s Touch Bar to take screenshots. Pressing the ‘Screen Capture’ button on the Touch Bar will open up the Grab application, allowing you to take a timed screen, selection, window, or full screen. You can also press this button and the ‘Shift’ key simultaneously to take a screenshot of the Touch Bar displayed content.

These three methods will assist Apple laptop users to easily capture their screen for whatever their needs may be. With these tips, you’ll be prepared to capture anything and everything on your laptop in no time!

3. Capturing with the Grab Tool#

  1. Capturing with the Grab Tool The Grab tool is a pre-installed tool on macOS-based Apple laptops that allows users to take different types of screenshots. To open the Grab tool, go to Finder > Applications > Utilities and look for the Grab application. Once open, users will be presented with four screenshot types: Selection, Window, Screen, and Timed Screen. After selecting the kind of screenshot, users can click one of the Capture buttons to execute. Using the Grab tool will allow users to get more advanced screenshot options, such as setting a timer to capture a full screen screenshot after a few seconds. The Grab tool also provides annotation options, such as drawing arrows and circles on the screenshot. By clicking File and then selecting Save, users can also save the screenshot to their laptop.

4. Capturing a Selected Area of Your Screen#

To capture a selected area of your screen, press Command + Shift + 4. Then move your mouse cursor to the area you want to capture. Click and drag to draw over the area you want to select, press Return or Enter when you are done. You can also hold the Space bar and click and drag to move the selected area around if you want to make a small change. After doing so, grab is automatically saved to your desktop.

Alternatively, you can press Command + Shift + 5 to open the Grab application. This gives you the option to choose how much of the screen you want to capture. You can select the entire screen, a window, or a selection of your choice. Once selected, you can take advantage of various tools for annotations and further modifications before saving the image.

If you need to take a screenshot of a single window such as a web browser, press Command + Shift + 4 and then press Space bar. Your mouse cursor will turn into a camera icon. Then move it over the window you want to capture and click on it. The screenshot will be automatically saved to your desktop.

Finally, if you want to record the video activity on your Apple laptop, open the Quick Time app and select File > New Screen Recording. Then select the area you want to capture and hit the Record button. When you’re done, go to File > Stop Screen Recording to save the video.
