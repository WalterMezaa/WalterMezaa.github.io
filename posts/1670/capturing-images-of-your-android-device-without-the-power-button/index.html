I. Introduction#

  1. To take screenshot of your Android device without power button, you will need to install Android Debug Bridge (ADB) in your PC. ADB is a command-line tool that allows you to do variety of tasks on your device from the PC.
  2. To install ADB correctly, you must configure your Windows system path. It is easy to configure the path by typing Environment Variables in Windows search and clicking Edit the system environment variables.
  3. In the System Properties window that opens, select the Advanced tab, then click Environment Variables. In the System Variables section, find the Path variable and click Edit.
  4. In the Edit System Variable, click New and type the full path to the SDK folder that you just downloaded. To make sure the path configures correctly, restart the computer.
  5. After that, open the Command Prompt window, and type adb devices for check your device is connected. If your device appears in the list, you are ready to take a screenshot of your device.
  6. Finally, to take the screenshot type in adb shell screencap -p /sdcard/screenshot.png and you get the for image saved in the sdcard of your device.

II. What You Need#

  1. Android Debug Bridge (ADB) provides a bridge for communication between a PC and Android device. ADB allows a variety of device actions and commands such as taking screenshots of an Android device without the power button.
  2. To take a screenshot of an Android device without the power button, you need to install the Android SDK on your PC. ADB is part of the SDK and you need to download the full package to have access to the ADB features.
  3. After you have installed the Android SDK, you need to make sure that the USB Debugging mode is enabled on your Android device. This setting is available in Developer options.
  4. Once USB Debugging is enabled, you need to connect your Android device to the PC via USB. After successful connection, you can open a Command Prompt window and enter “ADB devices” to check if the connection is working properly.
  5. The next step is to enter the command “adb shell screencap” to start capturing the screenshot of your Android device. Your screenshot will be saved in the SD card of your device.
  6. You can use the “adb pull” command to transfer the captured screenshot from the Android device to your PC. This command will allow you to quickly and easily access the screenshot on your PC.

III. Step by Step Guide#

  1. Begin by installing the Android SDK on your computer. Head to the official website and download the ADT Bundle to get the ADB components.

  2. After the ADB components have been installed, connect your Android device to your computer. You may need to install the right drivers to make the connection.

  3. After your device is connected, open a command prompt window in the same folder as your ADB tools. Then enter the following command to start the ADB server: adb start-server.

  4. Once the ADB server is running, you can use the adb devices command to display a list of connected devices. Ensure that the device you are looking to take screenshots from is listed.

  5. To take a screenshot, use the adb shell screencap command. This will take a full-screen screenshot and store it locally on your device.

  6. If you wish to transfer the screenshot over to your computer, you can do so by using the adb pull command. This will copy the file over to the folder specified in the command line.
