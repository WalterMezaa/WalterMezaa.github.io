This article will provide you with an easy-to-follow guide on how to take a screenshot on Android without using the power button. Let’s get started!

  1. Use the Notification Tray - Taking a screenshot on Android without using the power button is as simple as swiping down the notification tray and tapping the screenshot icon. According to Google, this method is available on Android 5.0 Lollipop and higher.

  2. Utilize the Volume Buttons - Similar to the notification tray, you can also access the screenshot feature by simultaneously pressing the volume down and power buttons. This method helps to capture a wide range of in-game screenshots or screenshots of long messages.

  3. Download Third-Party Apps - For devices running on lower Android versions, there are several third-party apps available for download such as Screenshot Easy and Screenshot Capture. Quoting a reputed mobile tech website, “these tools can be used to take screenshots on smartphones running Android 2.3 or higher.”

  4. Set Up Google Assistant - Google Assistant can be used to quickly capture a screenshot, but it has to be activated beforehand. It can also be used to send customized automated responses to pre-specified messages.

  5. Use Manufacturer-Specific Tools - Most Android device manufacturers equip their devices with unique screenshot tools. For instance, Samsung devices have the Palm Swipe feature that allows the user to take a screenshot by swiping their palm from one side of the display to the other. Similarly, Huawei devices have the Knuckle Screenshot feature.
