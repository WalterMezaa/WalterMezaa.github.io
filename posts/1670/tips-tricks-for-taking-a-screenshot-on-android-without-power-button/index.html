How to Take Screenshots on Android Without Using the Power Button Taking screenshots on Android can be a bit of a hassle, especially if you don’t have a power button. But don’t worry, here’s a quick guide on how to take screenshots without the need of a power button:

  1. Use a Third-Party App: There are plenty of third-party apps available in the Google Play Store that allow you to take screenshots without the need of a power button. All you need to do is install the app on your device, launch it, and take the screenshot.

  2. Use the Volume and Home Buttons: If you don’t want to use a third-party app, you can also take screenshots with the combination of the volume down button and the home button. Simply press and hold both buttons at the same time, and the screen will be captured.

  3. Use Google Assistant: Another great way to take screenshots without a power button is to use Google Assistant. Just open up Google Assistant and say “take a screenshot” and it will do exactly that.

  4. Use Gestures: If your Android device is running Android 9.0 Pie or higher, you can use the gesture navigation feature to take screenshots. All you need to do is go to your Settings > System > Gestures > System navigation. Then, select the “Gestures” option and turn it on. After that, you can simply swipe up with three fingers to take screenshots.

So, there you have it - four simple ways to take screenshots on Android without needing a power button. Whether you prefer using a third-party app, the volume and home buttons, Google Assistant or the gestures feature, you’ll be able to take screenshots without any hassle.
