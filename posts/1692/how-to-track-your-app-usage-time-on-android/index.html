  1. Access Built-in Usage Statistics Android offers a simple way to view an overview of how much time you spend using specific apps. To access this information: • Open your device’s Settings app and tap Digital Wellbeing & parental controls. • Scroll down and tap App Timers. • Here, you’ll be able to see a list of your recently used apps and how much time you have spent using each of them.

  2. Set Time Limits If you want to limit how much time you spend on a specific app, you can set time limits using the App Timer feature in Digital Wellbeing & parental controls. To do this: • Open Settings and tap Digital Wellbeing & parental controls. • Tap App Timers and select the app you want to limit. • Tap Set time limit and drag the clock hands to determine how much time you want to spend on the app.

  3. Use Third-Party Apps If you want more detailed and comprehensive data on your app usage, you can use a third-party app. There are several great apps that offer comprehensive tracking features, including Moment, QualityTime and Forest.

I. Prerequisites#

  1. To begin with, you should prepare for tracking your app usage time on Android. The OS version of your device needs to be Android 5.0 or higher.

  2. You also need to activate Android’s built-in app usage stats by going to your Android Device Settings and tapping Digital Wellbeing & parental controls.

  3. Then, turn on the ‘Usage access’ option. This would allow tracking and logging of the app usage.

  4. In the same menu, tap on ‘Show your data’. This will take you to Digital Wellbeing & parental controls and allow you to view app usage time stats.

  5. You can track your app usage data from the last day, week, month or your daily average. Additionally, you can also set time limits, or create a wind-down schedule.

  6. Lastly, be sure to enable the ‘Reminder’ setting so that you will know when you reaches your daily time limits.

II. Setting App Usage Time Limit#

This feature is built into Android operating systems and called App Usage Stats. To track your app usage time and set time limits, go to your phone’s Settings > Digital Wellbeing. Here you will be able to access the Digital Wellbeing dashboard, which provides users with insights on their app usage.

The dashboard shows you how often and for how long you are using an app. You can also set a daily time limit. If you exceed that limit, your phone will give you gentle reminders and eventually pause the app temporarily until the usage limit is reset the next day.

Aside from the app timer, Digital Wellbeing also offers Focus mode, a feature that lets you pause distracting apps so that you can focus on the task at hand. It is also possible to configure Bedtime mode and Wind Down mode, which will gradually reduce the amount of blue light emitting from your device.

If you have a child or family member using your Android device, you can also take advantage of the Family Link feature to set app activity limits and other parental controls. This will allow you to choose which apps and websites are accessible to them and how much they can use them.

Overall, the App Usage Stats feature provides a convenient way of tracking your time spent on each app and setting time limits as needed. It is a useful tool to help you stay focused and productive, or avoid spending too much time on social media.

III. Track Usage Time#

Tracking your usage time of each application on your Android device is very easy. To do this, simply head to the ‘Settings’ menu and select ‘Digital Wellbeing & Parental Controls’. If you have a Huawei device, you will find the setting titled ‘Digital Balance’. Next, select the ‘Usage’ option to track your app usage time. Once you have accessed the usage page, you can quickly see the amount of time you are spending on each application. Android also provides you with the ability to set daily limits for each application so that you are aware of how much time you’re spending on each app. Additionally, you can set up reminders to notify you when you’re close to using the app for a set amount of time. Lastly, you can rate your app usage so that you can easily identify which apps are consuming most of your time.
