Explaining the Step by Step Guide on How to Take Screenshot on Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus Taking screenshots with a Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus smartphone can be a very useful tool for capturing and sharing important information. This guide will walk you through how to take a screenshot with your Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus.

Step 1: Locate the Power/Lock and Volume Down buttons. On the right side of the Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus, you will find two buttons: the Power/Lock button and the Volume Down button.

Step 2: Press and hold both buttons simultaneously. Press and hold both the Power/Lock button and the Volume Down button at the same time. After a few seconds, you should see an animation and hear a sound indicating that a screenshot has been taken.

Step 3: Find and view the screenshot. Once a screenshot has been taken, the image will be saved in the Gallery app. You can access the Gallery app by tapping the icon located on the home screen. From within the Gallery app, you will be able to view, edit, or share the screenshot.

Capturing screenshots with your Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus is simple and easy. With just a few simple steps, you can capture important images on your smartphone in no time.

2. Taking Screenshots Using the Physical Buttons#

Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus owners can take screenshots by holding down the Power/Lock and Volume Down buttons simultaneously. It is important to time the button presses correctly and to make sure both buttons are pressed for roughly the same duration. The screenshot should then show up in your device’s Gallery application. To make a ‘long screenshot’, hold the Volume Down and Power/Lock buttons and wait for the scrolling capture animation to appear. Then, keep scrolling and capturing until you reach the end of the page. All screenshots are then combined into one long image and stored in your Gallery application. To take a screenshot quickly, swipe down from the top-right or top-left corner of your phone. This expands the quick settings menu and a ‘Screenshot’ button will appear at the top of the menu. Click the button to take the screenshot. Finally, you can capture a screenshot by using the ‘Smart Select’ feature. This can be accessed by swiping down with three fingers to open the Edge Panel and selecting the ‘Smart Select’ option. You can then choose from various shapes and screenshot sizes and edit the screenshot before saving it.

3. Using Palm Swipe to Capture#

Aside from pressing the Power/Lock and Volume Down buttons simultaneously, users of the Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus can also take screenshots using the Palm Swipe feature. To do so, the user will need to have enabled Palm Swipe in the Motion section of the Settings menu. They should then place their hand horizontally across the screen of their device until a translucent gray frame appears, after which they should simply swipe their hand from one side of the screen to the other. The screenshot will then be instantly captured and saved to the user’s Gallery. Additionally, a small animation will play on the device’s screen and a sound file will play, both providing audio-visual confirmations that the screenshot was successful.

4. Editing Screenshots on Samsung Galaxy S22 Plus#

Firstly, unlock the Samsung S22 Plus screen. Then, press and hold the Power/Lock key and Volume Down button simultaneously. To edit the screenshots on the device, open the Gallery and tap on the screenshot that you want to edit. This will open the image in the editing mode. Here, you can add various filters and other editing options like crop, rotate, draw, and stickers, etc. After making the edits on the screenshot, tap on the share button present at the top right corner to save or share the edited screenshot.
