Whether it’s an image, a web page, an error message, or something else, taking a screenshot can help you to capture the information quickly and easily. Here are some simple steps to take a screenshot on your ThinkPad Lenovo laptop:

  1. Press the ‘Fn’ key and ‘PrtScn’ (or ‘PrtSc’) key at the same time. This will capture a screenshot of the entire screen.

  2. To capture only a specific window, press the ‘Alt’ key and ‘PrtScn’ (or ‘PrtSc’) keys at the same time. This will capture a screenshot of only the window that is currently active.

  3. To capture a specific area of the screen, press the ‘Shift’ and ‘PrtScn’ (or ‘PrtSc’) keys at the same time. This will allow you to select the area of the screen that you want to capture.

  4. Once the screenshot is captured, you can save it to your computer. You can also paste the screenshot into an image editor, such as Paint or Photoshop, to edit or further enhance it.

By following these simple steps, you can easily take screenshots on your ThinkPad Lenovo laptop.

  1. Enable the PrtScr Key. If your ThinkPad Lenovo laptop did not come with the PrtScr Key pre-enabled, you will need to enable it in the BIOS. To do this, press the power button and the small ‘F2’ button simultaneously upon startup and then navigate to the ‘Function Key Behavior’ area of the BIOS. Here, you can select whether you want the functions activated upon pressing the ‘Fn’ button first, or upon pressing the ‘Fn’ button and the Prt Scr keys simultaneously.

  2. Capture the Entire Screen. After you have enabled the Function Keys, all you need to do is press the ‘Fn’ and PrtScr buttons simultaneously to capture the entire screen. If you wish to capture the currently active window, press the ‘Fn’, ‘Alt’ and ‘PrtScr’ buttons simultaneously instead.

  3. Locate the Captured Image. The captured image should save directly to your laptop’s clipboard. To locate and save the image from your laptop’s clipboard, open a simple document editor such as ‘Notepad’ or ‘MS Word’ and press the ‘Ctrl’ and ‘V’ keys simultaneously. This should paste the image directly into the document for you to save or edit as you see fit.

  4. Copy the Captured Image Directly to a Folder. For your convenience, you may want to save the captured image directly to a specified folder. To do this, simply press the ‘Windows’ and ‘Prt Scr’ buttons simultaneously instead of the ‘Fn’ and Prt Scr keys. This will enable you to save the captured image directly to your specified folder without having to paste it into a document.

  5. Utilize the Snipping Tool. This is an alternate method to capture screen images on a ThinkPad Lenovo laptop. To access the Snipping Tool, simply type ‘Snipping Tool’ in the Windows search box and then open it. Using this tool, you can specify a region of the screen you want to capture and then save the image as you like. This provides you with more options to customize your images.

For instance, pressing the ‘Fn + Prt Sc’ keys simultaneously will capture the entire screen, the ‘Fn + Alt + Prt Sc’ keys will capture the active window and the ‘Windows + Prt Sc’ keys will save the image directly to a folder.
