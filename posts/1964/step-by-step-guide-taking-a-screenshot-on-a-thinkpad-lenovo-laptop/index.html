1. Explain What a Screenshot Is#

A screenshot is a digital image of the content that is currently appearing on your device’s screen. It is useful for capturing information that you can refer back to later or even share with someone else. Screenshots are also helpful for troubleshooting as it allows others to see exactly what the visual output of your device is at any given time. Taking a screenshot is quick and easy and this guide will walk you through taking a screenshot on a ThinkPad Lenovo Laptop.

2. Describe the Different Ways to Take a Screenshot on a ThinkPad Lenovo Laptop#

There are multiple ways to take screenshots on a ThinkPad Lenovo laptop. The most common method is to press the PrtScn key which is located on the upper right part of the keyboard. This will take a screenshot of the entire screen. To capture an image of only the active window, press the “Fn” key and “PrtScn” key together. To take a screenshot of a specific area, press “Alt” and “PrtScn” key together. This allows users to select the particular area they want to capture. After taking a screenshot, open a program or image to paste and save the screenshot. For example, a user can open an image-editing program such as Paint and paste the screenshot there for saving the image.

3. Describe How to Take a Full-Screen Screenshot on a ThinkPad Lenovo Laptop#

Once you have located the Print Screen button on your ThinkPad Lenovo Laptop, open the program or image you wish to screenshot. Press the Print Screen button to capture the current screen.

To take a full-screen screenshot on your ThinkPad Lenovo Laptop, press the “Fn” key and “Print” key at the same time. This will capture the entire screen in an image.

For screenshots including the mouse cursor, press the “Fn” key and “Alt” key at the same time, then hit the “Print” button. This will capture the entire screen, including the mouse cursor.

If you only want to capture a screenshot of the active window, press the “Alt” key and “Print” key together. This will capture the active window.

If you wish to take a screenshot of the entire screen, including multiple windows or programs, press the “Fn” key and “Ctrl” key together, then press the “Print” button. This will capture the entire screen, including any open windows or programs.

You can also take screenshots using the built-in “Snipping Tool”. To use this method, open the “Snipping Tool”, select the area you would like to capture and click the “Save” button. This will save the screenshot as an image file.

4. Explain How to Take a Custom-Size Screenshot on a ThinkPad Lenovo Laptop#

To take a custom-size screenshot, open the image or program you would like to capture and press the Alt and Print Screen keys at the same time. This will capture the currently active window instead of the whole screen. After that, you can open Microsoft Paint, press Ctrl+V to paste the screenshot, then resize it as you want. Additionally, you can open any image editing software to edit the screenshot further, such as adding texts or color filters. Finally, save the screenshot in an image format of your choosing.
