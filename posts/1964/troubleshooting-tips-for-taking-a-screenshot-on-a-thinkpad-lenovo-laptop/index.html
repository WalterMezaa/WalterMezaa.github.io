1. Identify what type of operating system your Lenovo ThinkPad laptop is running.#

To identify what type of operating system your laptop is running, click the Windows icon in the bottom left corner of the taskbar. On the left side of the window, click System. This will display the system information with the list of operating system, including the version and build number. Alternatively, you can press the Windows key and Pause/Break key on your keyboard. It will take you to the same System window. If you’re not sure what type of operating system is installed, you can also visit the manufacturer’s website and search for the corresponding model number for the type of operating system. In addition, you can check the product page of the laptop to find out which Windows version it is running.

2. Enable the screenshot function based on the operating system.#

For Windows 8 or later, you can take a screenshot directly from the Windows Snipping Tool. Open the start menu, find the Snipping tool, and select “New” to take your screenshot. You may also use the “Delay” feature to capture a specific part of the screen. For Mac users, you can press “Command” + “Shift” + “4” to take a screenshot. Windows 10 users can also press the “Windows” + “Print Screen” keys to take a screenshot, and the image will be automatically saved in the “Screenshots” folder. If you want to capture an active window, you can press “Alt” + “Print Screen”. For Linux users, the action of taking a screenshot is a bit different. To take a screenshot of the entire screen, press “Ctrl” + “Alt” + “Print Screen”. If you want to capture the active window, press “Alt” + “Print Screen”. Then, the screenshot will be saved to the “Pictures” folder of your Home directory. Lastly, you can open the screenshot in an image editor like GIMP or Paint to edit and save it.

3. Take the screenshot according to the instructions of the operating system.#

Most of the screenshots can be used to provide visual evidence of the computer issue or bug. Taking a screenshot is an important step in troubleshooting a problem as it gives support personnel a look at the actual state of the computer at that moment. To take a screenshot on a Lenovo ThinkPad laptop, press the Fn and PrtSc keys simultaneously. This will save a copy of the entire laptop screen to the clipboard. Then open an image editor like Microsoft Paint or Adobe Photoshop, press Ctrl and V keys to paste the screenshot. You can also use the screenshot snipping tool in Windows 10 to create a screenshot with limited capture area.

4. Save the screenshot to the required location.#

When you take a screenshot, the image is saved as a .png file in the Screenshots folder, which is located in the Pictures folder. You can rename the file and save it to any other location like a Windows or cloud storage folder. To save it to a different location, select Save as from the File menu. Then, choose the location and hit the “Save” button. Additionally, you can also select a different file format like .jpg or .gif for image.

Q. What is the best way to take a screenshot on a ThinkPad Lenovo laptop? A. Pressing the “PrtSc” or “PrtScn” key on the top right of the keyboard is the simplest and most common way to take a screenshot on a ThinkPad Lenovo laptop. The “PrtSc” or “PrtScn” key is usually located on the top right of the keyboard, just below the “F12” key.

Q. How do I find the screenshot I just took on my ThinkPad Lenovo laptop? A. Screenshots taken on ThinkPad Lenovo laptops are usually saved in the “Screenshots” folder located in the Pictures library. If you can’t find it, simply search for the file name “Screenshot (number).png” in the search box located in the taskbar.

Q. What if I want to take a screenshot of only a certain area of the screen? A. To take a screenshot of only a certain area of the screen, press the “Windows Key + Shift + S” to bring up the “Snip & Sketch” tool. From there, you can click and drag to select the area of the screen you would like to capture.

Q. Is there a way to take a screenshot without using the keyboard? A. Yes, you can also use the “Windows Key + Volume Down” key combination to take a screenshot. This will take a picture of the entire screen and save it to the “Screenshots” folder.

Q. What if I want to annotate or edit my screenshot? A. You can use the “Snip & Sketch” tool to edit and annotate screenshots on ThinkPad Lenovo laptops. All you have to do is press the “Windows Key + Shift + S” key combination to bring up the “Snip & Sketch” window, and then you can add text, draw, and highlight areas of the screenshot.
